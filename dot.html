What is a DOT Physical?

A DOT physical is a federally mandated health examination required for commercial vehicle drivers. It ensures you're physically, mentally, and emotionally fit to operate a commercial motor vehicle safely. Only certified medical examiners, like the healthcare professionals at Better With IJ, can perform this exam and issue the necessary Medical Examiner's Certificate (MEC).

What to Expect During Your DOT Physical

During your DOT exam, our certified examiner will evaluate the following:

  • Vision and hearing
  • Blood pressure and pulse rate
  • Urinalysis (for underlying conditions like diabetes)
  • Review of medical history and medications
  • Physical examination of multiple systems (lungs, neurological, musculoskeletal, etc.)

How to Prepare

To make your visit smooth, please bring the following:

  • Valid driver's license
  • List of current medications
  • Glasses or hearing aids (if used)
  • Any medical documentation related to chronic conditions (e.g., CPAP reports, cardiac clearances, etc.)

If you're unsure whether your health history will affect your certification, call us ahead of your appointment. We’re happy to help you prepare.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a DOT physical take?

Most DOT exams take about 30–45 minutes, depending on your health history and how prepared you are.

How often do I need to get a DOT physical?

DOT medical certificates are typically valid for up to 2 years. However, drivers with certain health conditions may need more frequent exams.

Can I still pass if I have high blood pressure or diabetes?

Yes, you can pass the DOT exam with controlled high blood pressure or diabetes. Bring relevant documentation and recent test results to help our provider assess your eligibility.

Is Better With IJ certified to provide DOT physicals?

Yes, our clinic has certified medical examiners who are listed on the National Registry and authorized to perform DOT physicals.
